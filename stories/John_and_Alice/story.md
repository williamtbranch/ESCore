John saw something. He saw it far from him. It was someone. It was a person. It was her. Alice. He wanted to be with her. To be with Alice. He felt good things. Good things when near her. When he was near her, he felt good things. He thought she felt good things. Good things when with him. With John. They felt good things. Good things when they were together. Alice and John did many things. Many things together. When they were with each other. Good things happened. At this time John wanted to go to her. To the place she was. The place he saw. At this time, he would go. Go today. Go now. When he saw her, he said something. He said it because he wanted to know something. He asked a question. He asked her the question. He asked, “Do you want to do something?” She heard him. She said, “I want to do something.”
Alice thought about it. She thought about doing something. Something with John. She wanted to do something. Something good. She thought about good things. The good things that happened. That happened when they were together. When they did things together.

John was happy. Happy because she said this. She said she wanted to do something. Something with him. With John. He thought about what they could do. What kind of thing they could do together. There were many things. Many things they could do.

"We could go somewhere," John said. He said this to her. To Alice. She looked at him. She thought about going somewhere. Going somewhere with John. Going to a place. A place where they could do things. Good things. Things they both wanted to do.

"Where could we go?" she asked. She asked this because she wanted to know. To know the place. The place John was thinking about. The place they could go together. She wanted to know what John thought. What he thought about this.

John knew a place. A good place. A place where people go. Where people do things. Where people are together. He thought Alice would like this place. Would like to be there. To be there with him. With John.
John said, "There is a place. A place where there is food. Where people eat food. Where they sit. Where they sit and eat. Together. We could go there. Go there and eat. Eat together."

Alice thought this was good. A good thing to do. To eat together. To be in this place together. She felt good things. Good things about this. About eating with John. About being with John. In this place.

"Yes," she said. She said this to tell him. To tell him she wanted this. Wanted to go to this place. This place with food. With him. With John. "I want to go there. I want to eat. To eat with you."

They went. They went to this place. The place with food. They walked. They walked together. When they walked, they were close. Close to each other. This felt good. Good to both of them. To Alice and to John.

At this place, there were other people. Other people doing things. Doing the same things. Sitting. Eating. Being together. Alice and John found a place. A place to sit. To sit together. They sat close. Close to each other. They looked at things. Things they could eat. Food they could put in their bodies. Food they wanted.
Alice saw something. She saw food she wanted. Food she wanted to eat. To put in her body. "I want this," she said. She said this to someone. To a person. A person who was there. A person who was not John. This person got food. Got food for people. For people who came to this place.

John saw something too. Something he wanted. Food he wanted to eat. He told this person. Told them what he wanted. What food he wanted to eat. The person went away. Went to get the food. The food Alice and John wanted.

They waited. Waited for their food. While they waited, they did something. They talked. They said things. Things to each other. John said things to Alice. Alice said things to John. This was good. Good to talk. To say things. To hear things. Things from each other.

The person came back. Came back with food. The food they wanted. The food was in front of them. In front of Alice. In front of John. They looked at their food. They felt good things. Good things about the food. About being there. About being together.

They ate. They put food in their bodies. While they ate, they looked at each other. They smiled. A smile is something faces do. Something faces do when people feel good things. Alice and John felt good things. They smiled at each other. They ate and smiled.
