It was a good day. There was a lot of sun. John and Alice went out together. They didn't have to do anything. They only wanted to be together.

They went slowly down the street. Alice looked at the sky. "Look," she said. "The sky is very pretty today." John looked at it too. "Yes," he said. "It's a good day."

They went to a place with water. There were big trees. There were people. Some children were doing things near the water. Everything was fine.

John and Alice found a place to sit. They sat together, one near the other. From there they could see the water. The sun was on the water. It was pretty to see.

Alice got very close to John. "I like being here," she said. "I like being with you." John felt something good. "Me too," he said.

Some time passed. They didn't say many things. They didn't have to say things. Being together was good. They saw the water. They saw the sun. They didn't want to be anywhere else.

Alice took John's hand. His hand was warm. This felt good. The two of them looked at the water and the sun. They wanted to be here, the two of them, without doing anything more.

"Do you want to eat something?" John said after a while. Alice thought a little. "A little," she said. "But I want to be here more time. This place makes me feel good." John smiled. "Fine," he said. "We can be here more. After, we'll eat something."

More time passed. The people came and went. Some went slowly. Others went fast. A small child stopped near them and looked at them. Alice smiled at him. The child smiled too and went off with his people.

"The people are happy today," Alice said. "Yes," John said. "I'm happy too." Alice looked at him. "Because of the day?" she said. "Not only because of the day," John said. "Because of being with you." Alice didn't say anything. She just got closer to him.

They were there a long time. The sun went down a little. Now the day was near the night. The sky changed. The two of them looked at it without saying anything. It was very pretty.

"We have to go soon," John said. "Yes," Alice said. "But it was a good day. One of the good ones." John took her hand again. "Yes," he said. "And there are going to be more days like this one." Alice smiled.

They sat a little more. Then they went off together, one near the other, away from the water. They didn't say many things. They didn't have to say things. They only wanted to be together, and that was good enough.
