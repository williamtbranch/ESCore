Little Red Riding Hood
Once there was a little girl. Everyone who saw her loved her, but her grandmother loved her most of all. One day her grandmother gave her a little red cap. The girl liked it so much that she wore it every day. So everyone called her Little Red Cap.
One morning her mother said to her, "Come here, Little Red Cap. Your grandmother is sick and weak. Here is some cake and a bottle of wine. Take them to her house. They will do her good."
"Yes, Mother," said the girl.
"Go now, before the day gets hot," said her mother. "Walk well and do not leave the path. If you leave the path, you can fall, and then your grandmother will get nothing. And when you enter her house, say 'good morning' to her."
"I will take great care," said Little Red Cap. And she gave her mother her hand.
The grandmother lived far away, out in the woods. When Little Red Cap entered the woods, a wolf came to her. The girl did not know that the wolf was a bad animal, so she was not afraid of him.
"Good day, Little Red Cap," said the wolf.
"Thank you, wolf," she said.
"Where are you going so early?" said the wolf.
"To my grandmother's house," said the girl.
"And what do you have there?" said the wolf.
"Cake and wine," she said. "My grandmother is sick and weak, and they will make her stronger."
"And where does your grandmother live, Little Red Cap?" said the wolf.
"Her house is farther on in the woods," said the girl. "It is under three big trees. You surely know it."
The wolf thought, "This little girl is good to eat. She will be better than the old woman. I must think of a plan, and then I can eat them both."
So the wolf walked with her for a little while. Then he said, "Look, Little Red Cap. Look at all the pretty flowers. Why do you not look around you? You do not hear the little birds singing. You walk on as if you were going to school, but here in the woods everything is happy."
Little Red Cap raised her eyes. She saw the sun dancing through the trees. She saw the pretty flowers everywhere. She thought, "I can take fresh flowers to my grandmother. She will like them. It is still early, so I will get there in good time."
So she left the path and went into the woods to look for flowers. And every time she took a flower, she saw a prettier one farther on, and she went after it. So she went deeper and deeper into the woods.
But the wolf ran straight to the grandmother's house. He knocked on the door.
"Who is there?" said the grandmother.
"Little Red Cap," said the wolf. "I am bringing you cake and wine. Open the door."
"Open it yourself," called the grandmother. "I am too weak to get up."
The wolf opened the door. He said nothing. He went straight to the grandmother's bed and he ate her. Then he put on her clothes and her cap, and he lay down in her bed.
After a while Little Red Cap remembered her grandmother. She had so many flowers now that she could carry no more. So she went on to the house.
She was surprised. The door was open. When she entered the room, she felt strange. She said to herself, "Oh dear. Today I feel afraid, and other days I like being with my grandmother so much."
She said, "Good morning," but no one answered. So she went to the bed and looked at her grandmother. Her grandmother looked very strange.
"Oh, grandmother," she said, "what big ears you have!"
"The better to hear you with, my child," said the wolf.
"But, grandmother, what big eyes you have!"
"The better to see you with, my dear."
"But, grandmother, what big hands you have!"
"The better to hold you with."
"But, grandmother, what a big mouth you have!"
"The better to eat you with!"
And the wolf jumped out of the bed and ate poor Little Red Cap.
Now the wolf was full. He lay down in the bed again and fell asleep, and he began to snore very loud. A hunter was passing the house. He thought, "How loud the old woman snores! I must see if she needs anything."
So he went into the room. When he came to the bed, he saw the wolf lying in it. "I find you here, you bad animal!" he said. "I have looked for you for a long time."
He wanted to kill the wolf. But then he thought, "Maybe the wolf ate the grandmother. Maybe I can still save her." So he did not kill him. He took a knife and cut open the wolf.
When he cut, he saw the little red cap. He cut a little more, and the little girl jumped out. "Oh, how afraid I was!" she said. "It was so dark inside the wolf!" Then the old grandmother came out too. She was alive, but she could hardly breathe.
Little Red Cap brought big, heavy stones. They filled the wolf with the stones. When the wolf woke up, he wanted to run away. But the stones were so heavy that he fell down and died.
Then all three were happy. The hunter took the wolf's skin and went home. The grandmother ate the cake and drank the wine, and she felt better. And Little Red Cap thought, "As long as I live, I will never leave the path and run into the woods, when my mother tells me not to."

Another time, Little Red Cap was again taking cake to her grandmother. Another wolf came to her and tried to take her from the path. But this time the girl was careful. She went straight on her way. She told her grandmother, "I met a wolf on the path. He said 'good morning' to me, but he had such bad eyes. If we had not been on the road, I think he would have eaten me."
"Well," said the grandmother, "then we will shut the door, so he cannot come in."
Soon the wolf knocked on the door. "Open the door, grandmother," he called. "I am Little Red Cap, and I am bringing you cake."
But they said nothing, and they did not open the door. So the wolf walked around the house. At last he jumped up on the roof. He wanted to wait there until evening, and then follow Little Red Cap home in the dark and eat her.
But the grandmother knew what he was thinking. In front of the house there was a big stone trough. So she said to the girl, "Take this water, Little Red Cap. Yesterday I cooked, and here is the water. Carry it to the trough."
Little Red Cap carried the water until the big trough was full. Then the smell came up to the wolf. He looked down. He stretched his neck so far that he could not hold on, and he began to slip. He slipped off the roof and fell straight into the trough, and there he died.
And Little Red Cap went home happy, and no one ever hurt her again.
