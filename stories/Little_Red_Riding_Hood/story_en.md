Little Red Riding Hood
There was once a little girl. Everyone who saw her loved her, but her grandmother loved her more than anyone. One day the old woman gave her a red cap. The girl liked it so much that she wore it every day. For that reason everyone called her Little Red Riding Hood.
%%META illustration: lrrh_mother_basket%%
One morning her mother said to her: "Come here, Little Red Riding Hood. Your grandmother is very sick and without strength. Here you have good food and water. Take them to her house. They will do her good."
"Yes, mother," said the girl.
"Go now, before it gets very sunny," said her mother. "Walk well and do not leave the path. If you leave the path, you can fall, and then your grandmother will have nothing. And when you arrive at her house, say 'good morning' to her."
"I will take much care," said Little Red Riding Hood. And she gave her hand to her mother.
The grandmother lived far away, in the forest. When Little Red Riding Hood entered the forest, a wolf came toward her.
%%META illustration: lrrh_meets_wolf%%
The girl did not know that the wolf was a bad animal, so she was not afraid of him.
"Good morning, Little Red Riding Hood," said the wolf.
"Thank you, wolf," she said.
"Where are you going so early/soon?", said he.
"To the house of my grandmother," said the girl.
"And what do you carry there?", said the wolf.
"Good food and water," said she. "My grandmother is very sick and without strength, and they will make her stronger."
"And where does the woman live, Little Red Riding Hood?", said the wolf.
"Her house is further ahead in the forest," said the girl. "It is under three big trees. Surely you know it."
The wolf thought: "This little girl is good to eat. She will be better than the old woman. I have to think of a plan, and then I can eat them both."
So he walked with her a little bit of time. Then he said: "Look, Little Red Riding Hood. Look at all the pretty flowers. Why do you not look around you? You do not hear the animals sing. You walk as if you were going to school, but here in the forest everything is happy."
Little Red Riding Hood raised her eyes. She saw the sun between the trees. She saw the pretty flowers everywhere. She thought: "I can take new flowers to my grandmother. She is going to like them. I still have much time, so I will arrive at a good hour."
So she left the path and entered the forest to look for flowers. And every time she took a flower, she saw another prettier one further ahead, and she went after it. Thus she went further and further away into the forest.
But the wolf ran straight to the grandmother's house. He knocked at the door.
"Who is there?", said the grandmother.
"Little Red Riding Hood," said the wolf. "I bring you good food and water. Open the door."
"Open it yourself," said the woman. "I am without strength to get up."
The wolf opened the door. He said nothing. He went straight to the bed of the woman and ate her. Then he put on her clothes and her cap, and went to his bed.
After a little bit of time Little Red Riding Hood remembered her grandmother. Now she had so many flowers that she could not carry more. So she continued toward the house.
The door was open. When she entered the room, she felt strange. She said to herself: "Today I have fear, and other days I like being with my grandmother so much."
She said: "Good morning", but no one answered. So she went to the bed and looked at the woman. She did not look like herself.
%%META illustration: lrrh_wolf_in_bed%%
"Oh, grandmother," she said, "you have very big ears."
"To hear you better, my child," said the wolf.
"But, grandmother, you have very big eyes."
"To see you better, dear."
"But, grandmother, you have very big hands."
"To hold you better."
"But, grandmother, you have a very big mouth."
"To eat you better!"
And the wolf came out of the bed and ate poor Little Red Riding Hood.
Now the wolf was full. He went again to the bed and fell asleep, and was heard very loud upon sleeping. A man was passing by the house. He thought: "How loud the old woman is heard upon sleeping! I have to see if she needs something."
So he entered the room. When he arrived at the bed, he saw him in it. "Here you are, bad animal!", he said. "I have looked for you a long time."
He wanted to kill the wolf. But then he thought: "Maybe the wolf ate the woman. Maybe I can still save her." So he did not kill him. He took something to cut and opened the wolf.
When he cut, he saw the red cap. He cut a little more, and the little girl came out. "What fear I had!", she said. "It was very black inside the wolf!" Then the old woman came out too. She was alive, but almost could not take air.
Little Red Riding Hood brought big and heavy stones. They filled the wolf with the stones. When he woke up, he wanted to run. But the stones were so heavy that he fell to the ground and died.
Then the three were very well. The man took the wolf and went home. The old woman ate the food and drank the water, and felt better. And Little Red Riding Hood thought: "As long as I live, I will never leave the path to run into the forest, when my mother tells me not to."

Another day, Little Red Riding Hood was carrying food to her grandmother. Another bad animal came toward her and tried to take her off the path. But this time the girl was careful. She continued straight on her path. She told her grandmother: "I saw a wolf on the path. He said 'good morning' to me, but he had very bad eyes. If we had not been on the street, I think he would have eaten me."
"Well," said the woman, "then we are going to close the door, so that he cannot enter."
Soon the wolf knocked at the door. "Open the door, grandmother," he said. "I am Little Red Riding Hood, and I bring you food."
But they said nothing, and they did not open the door. So the wolf walked around the house. Finally he went up, on top of the house. He wanted to wait there until night, and then follow Little Red Riding Hood to her house in the night and eat her.
But the woman knew what he was thinking. In front of the house there was a big place of stone to put water. So she said to the girl: "Take this water, Little Red Riding Hood. A day ago I made food, and here is the water. Carry it there."
Little Red Riding Hood carried the water until the place of stone was full. Then the wolf smelled the food.
%%META illustration: lrrh_wolf_falls_roof%%
He looked down. He moved his head so far that he could not continue up, and began to fall. He fell from the house straight to the water, and there he died.
And Little Red Riding Hood went home happy, and no one ever hurt her again.
