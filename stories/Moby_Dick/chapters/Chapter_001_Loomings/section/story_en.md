## ch01_call_me_ishmael

Call me Ishmael. Some time ago —it does not matter how much— I had very little
money, and nothing here made me want to stay. So I thought: I am going to go out
to sea for a while and see that part of the world that is only water. I do this
when I am not well inside. There are times when everything looks without life. I
do not want to say anything to anyone. I stop at the places where they keep the
dead, and I walk behind the people who cry. On those days I feel something very
bad inside, something so big that I want to do bad things in the places where
people go, things that people do not want and that I would do without thinking
well. When this happens, I know it is time to go to sea, as soon as I can. Other
people, when they feel like this, do worse things to themselves. Not me. I just
take a ship, without saying anything. This is how it is. If they thought about it
well, almost all men, at some moment, feel about the sea almost the same as I do.

## ch01_manhattan_waterfront

Look at the city next to the water, that big place where many people live. The
sea is on all its sides. Many roads, going one way and another, take you toward
the sea. On one side of the city, down below, the water reaches the land and the
sea air arrives good and new; that same air, a while before, was far away, with
no land near. On the water side there are many people looking at the sea, looking
as far as they can see. Some are standing. Others are not. Others look at the
boats without saying anything. They all come from their houses and from the
places where they do what they do to live. But they always arrive at the same
place: where the water begins. They come from everywhere. And there they are, as
if they were waiting for something.
