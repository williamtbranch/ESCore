Chapter 1. Loomings.
Say my name is Ishmael.
A time before now.
It does not matter how long.
I had very few things.
I had nothing I wanted to do.
So I thought I would go to the sea.
The sea is the greatest of all waters.
I wanted to go for a short time.
I wanted to see the watery part of the world.
I do this when I want to feel good.
When everything inside me feels dark.
Like a cold, wet day with no sun.
When I walk to every place where they keep the dead.
And most of all, when I feel very bad.
So bad that I want to do bad things.
Bad things to the people I pass in the street.
Then I know it is time to go to the sea.
Time to go not a long time from now.
Going to the sea is what I do so I do not do worse things.
Other men do bad things to their own bodies.
I make no sound.
I go to the ship.
A ship is a big house that moves on the water.
I go to the ship.
There is nothing strange about this.
If they only knew it.
Almost all men feel this way.
At some time, they feel the same way I do about the sea.
Look at the city.
The city is a big place of many houses and streets.
The water is all around the city.
The streets all take you toward the water.
Go down to the edge.
Where the water moves against the stones.
Look at all the people standing there.
Looking out at the sea.
Walk through the city in the middle of the day.
A quiet day when little is happening.
When the mind goes where it wants on its own.
Go from one end of the water to the other.
What do you see?
People.
Many, many people.
Standing still.
Looking out at the ocean.
Lost in their own thoughts.
Some stand against the wood.
Some sit at the edge of the water.
Some look out at the ships.
But all of these are men of the land.
On other days they are shut inside.
Kept at their work.
Not free to leave.
So what is this?
Why are they all here at the water?
And look, more of them come.
Walking straight toward the water.
As if they want to fall in.
Nothing makes them happy but the edge of the land.
The place where water and land touch.
They come from everywhere.
From the north and the south, the east and the west.
Yet here they all come together, at the water.
Tell me.
What is it about the sea that brings them here?
Think about it another way.
Say you are out in the country.
Where the land moves up into mountains.
And the waters are small.
Take any path you want.
Almost always, it will take you down to water.
To a pool or a small river.
There is something in this.
Take a man who is deep in his own thoughts.
Put him on his feet.
Let him walk.
He will lead you to water.
If there is any water at all in that place.
People and water belong together.
Everyone knows this.
When a man wants to think, he goes to the water.
When a man wants to feel, he goes to the water.
Now, when I say I go to sea.
I do not mean I go as a passenger.
A passenger is a person on a ship who pays money and does no work.
To go as a passenger, you need money.
I had no money.
And besides, passengers, after some time, do not feel well on the water.
Their bodies feel bad.
They do not sleep well.
They are not happy.
No, I never go as a passenger.
And I do not go as a captain.
The captain is the big man who tells everyone on the ship what to do.
I do not go as a captain.
And I do not go as a cook.
The cook is the man who makes food for everyone.
I do not go as any kind of leader.
I leave that to the men who want it.
For me, it is enough work just to take care of myself.
I do not want to take care of a whole ship.
When I go to sea, I go as a simple sailor.
A sailor is a man who works on a ship with his hands.
I work down near the front of the ship.
True, they tell me what to do.
They move me here and there all day.
At first, this does not feel good.
A man feels small because of it.
Especially if he comes from a good family.
Or if, before this, he was a big man.
A teacher, say, who told words to boys.
Boys who thought he was important.
To go from that to a sailor is a hard change.
But after a while, the bad feeling goes away.
And what of it?
If an old captain tells me to wash the wood of the ship.
What does that really matter?
Does it make me less of a man?
Everyone, in some way, is told what to do by someone else.
We are all moved about, one way or another.
So I do my work as a sailor, and it is fine with me.
And there is this.
When I go as a sailor, they pay me.
They give me money.
They never pay a passenger.
The passenger has to pay them.
There is a big difference between paying and being paid.
Giving money away is one of the hardest things in life.
But being given money...
There is nothing like it.
And one more thing.
As a sailor, I live in the good air at the front of the ship.
Why, after all my times at sea on trading ships...
Did I now decide to go on a ship that hunts whales?
A whale, oh! That great animal of the sea.
Why did I want to go on a voyage to find them?
The voyage being a long time moving on the sea.
I cannot fully say.
Something I cannot see seems to move me.
It seems to watch over me.
And to lead me where it wants.
I think this whaling voyage was always going to happen to me.
It was written for me a long time ago.
A small part in a much larger story.
Most of all, what moved me was the thought of the great whale himself.
Such a huge and strange animal.
It made me want to see it for myself.
I thought of the far-off seas.
Where he moves his mountain-sized body.
Of the many bad things that have no name.
Other men might not want such things.
But I am a man who always wants what is far away.
I love to sail seas where no one should sail.
And to land on strange shores.
So the whaling voyage was welcome.
The great doors of a world full of new things opened for me.
And through them came many whales.
Two by two.
And in the middle of them all...
One great white shape.
Like a hill of snow standing in the air.
