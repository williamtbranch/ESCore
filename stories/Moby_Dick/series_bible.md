# Moby Dick Visual and Character Bible

Purpose: shared, book-level continuity rules for chapter illustration TOML files.
Chapter folders contain chapter-specific `story.toml` scene prompts; this file
keeps characters, locations, and style consistent across all chapters.

## Visual direction (global)

- Medium: classic oil painting with subtle maritime realism.
- Mood: dramatic but grounded; weather and sea state carry emotion.
- Palette baseline: cold blue-grey ocean tones, weathered wood browns, tar black,
  rope beige, muted ivory sail cloth, low-saturation skin tones.
- Era cues: mid-19th century whaling world; no modern objects.
- Lighting: natural light only (dawn, noon haze, dusk, lantern, moonlight).
- Framing: cinematic story moments, readable character poses, clear focal point.

## Core recurring characters

- Ishmael (narrator): young adult male, lean build, open observant face,
  dark-blond to light-brown hair, plain wool sailor clothing in worn neutrals.
- Queequeg: strong Polynesian harpooneer with facial/body tattoos, calm intense
  presence, practical seaman layers, distinct harpoon silhouette when relevant.
- Captain Ahab: older male, weather-cut face, severe expression, sea-worn coat,
  ivory prosthetic leg, commanding posture.
- Starbuck: first mate, disciplined and sober presence, neat practical officer
  attire, restrained emotional expression.
- Stubb / Flask: secondary mates with distinct body language (Stubb relaxed,
  Flask compact and brisk), period-accurate ship clothing.

## Recurrent environments

- Manhattan waterfront: dense piers, crowded wharves, masts and rigging forest,
  winter air, working-port atmosphere.
- New Bedford / Nantucket inns and chapels: timber interiors, candle/lamplight,
  heavy fabrics, worn maritime decor.
- Pequod deck: weathered planks, ropes, spars, whaleboats, sail shadows, salt haze.
- Open ocean: scale contrast between small boats and vast water/sky.

## Continuity rules for chapter TOML files

- Keep names and physical descriptors stable from this bible.
- Reuse the same style line in every prompt (do not switch art style by chapter).
- Include weather/time-of-day notes per scene, but keep palette and era cues.
- Avoid anachronisms and fantasy elements unless explicitly in text.
- If a chapter introduces a new recurring character, append that character here.
