He's Not Daniel
Episode One

Far from all the large places, there is a small place.
The people who live here know who all the people from here are.
Nothing large happens in this place, and the people want it that way.
Rosa lives here with Inés, a person who has lived a very, very long time, and who knows many things — perhaps more things than people think.


A long time before, someone lived in this place — Daniel. Rosa was always at his side. When she was near him, she felt something very good inside her body, and she wanted to be with him all the time of her living. But Daniel wanted to see the large places and do large things, and he went far from here. After a long time, words came to the small place: Daniel is dead. Rosa heard these words, and something inside her died too.

Now, in this moment, someone comes to the place. The people see him and cannot say a word. His body is like the body of Daniel. He moves like Daniel moved. When people say to him: "Daniel? Are you Daniel?", he says: "No. I am not Daniel. I am Marco."

Inés sees Marco from far away, and her body feels something very bad. She says to Rosa: "Hear my words. Do not be near that person. Do not say words to him. I know something about him — something from a long time before. If you knew it, you would not be able to live as before." Rosa very much wants to know this thing. But Inés says only: "No. Some words can do bad things to people. I do not want to say more."

After this the night comes. In the night, all the people of the place are inside. In the night, Marco comes near the place where Rosa lives. He says small words to her — very small words. Words that only Daniel knew. Words that Daniel said to Rosa a long time before, words that she never said to anyone else. Rosa cannot move. She thinks: People said that Daniel is dead. But did anyone see him die? Then Marco says: "You know who I am. And perhaps you know why I am here."

After Marco said these words, Rosa could not feel well. She did not want to see people; she did not want to hear people. The small words moved inside her all the time, like something alive. She thought: If this person is not Daniel, how can he know these words? And if he is Daniel — why does he say that he is not? After a time, she knew one thing: she had to see him. She had to hear more.

Rosa went to the place where Marco was. She said to him: "Only one person knew these words, and people say that that person is dead. Who are you?" Marco was near her and said nothing for a time. Then he said: "Perhaps a person can be dead and not dead. Perhaps a person dies because other people want him dead. Perhaps a person dies because someone says: he is dead — and all the people think that these words are true." Rosa said: "I do not know what you are saying. Say it with small words." And Marco said: "Not now. Perhaps later. Now it is not good for you to know it."

In this place lives Tomás. When Daniel was small, Tomás lived at his side; the two were like two parts of one thing, and people said: where one is, the other is. When the words came to this place, a long time before — Daniel is dead — it was Tomás who said them before all the other people. He said: "I was far from here, in a large place, and the people of that place told me." Now Tomás sees Marco, and something very bad happens inside his body. Not because Marco is like Daniel. It is because of something Tomás did, a long time before — something that no one here knows.

In that same time, Tomás went to where Inés was. He said to her: "You see what I see. If this person is Daniel, you know what can happen to me." Inés said: "I know what you did, Tomás. I knew it before all of this. But hear my words: I do not want you to do something bad to this person. If you do something bad to him now, all the people are going to know what you did." Tomás said: "And if I do nothing?" Inés said nothing more.

That night, Inés went to the place where Marco was, moving like a person who has lived a very, very long time. She said: "A long time before, you said to me: I am never going to come to this place. For a long time I said nothing to anyone. Why did you come now?" And Marco said: "Because now I know what happened. I know what Tomás did. And I know, Inés, that you saw it all — and said nothing."

Rosa saw Inés when she was coming from the place where Marco was. She saw something in her: Inés was moving like a person who has something very large inside the body. Rosa went toward her and said to her: "You went to see him. I saw you. What did he say to you?" Inés did not want to talk. But Rosa said more: "You know things, Inés. You know things about Marco. You know things about Daniel. And you tell me nothing. I am not small. I want to know the truth."

Inés looked at her for a long time. Then she said: "There are words that do bad things, Rosa. When you hear them, you cannot live as before. Your life is one thing now; after the words, it is another thing. Do you want that?" And Rosa said: "Yes. I want it. Because living like this — not knowing, thinking all the time, feeling this inside the body — is like being dead. I want the truth, Inés. I want it more than all things." Inés said, with very small words: "You are like him. Daniel used to say things like that too." After a time, she said more: "I am going to tell you one thing — one thing, no more. When Daniel left this place, he did not leave alone. Tomás left with him. The two left on the same night. And after a long time, Tomás came back here — alone." Rosa could not move. She said: "But Tomás says that he was far away, and that the people of a large place told him that Daniel…" "Yes," said Inés. "That is what he says." And she said no more.

Rosa thought a great deal about Inés's words. The two left, on the same night. Tomás came back — alone. And it was Tomás who said: Daniel is dead. She thought: I want to hear what Tomás says. I want to see his body when he talks.

Rosa went to the place where Tomás lives. She said to him: "Tomás, you said the words, a long time before. You said: Daniel is dead. How did you know it? Who told you? Did you see his body?" Tomás was not looking at her when he spoke. He was looking at something else — something far away, something that was not here. He said: "I was far away, in a large place. The people of that place told me. I did not see his body. But I know it is true." Rosa said: "How can you know it is true, if you did not see it?" And Tomás said, with large words: "Because I know it! People do not say things like that when they are not true!" But his body said something else. His body moved a great deal; he could not be still. Rosa saw this and thought: You left with him, Tomás. Inés knows it. I know it. Why do you say these things? But she said nothing of this. Something inside her said: not now.

On another night, Tomás went to the place where Marco was. He went like a person who does not want people to see him. Marco was in that place, like a person who knows who is coming before seeing them. Tomás said: "Why did you come to this place? What do you want?" Marco did not move. He said: "I want to see this place. I want to see the people. I want to see how a person lives when he thinks: no one knows what I did." Tomás felt something very bad inside the body. He said: "If you are Daniel, say it now." And Marco said: "And if I am? What do you do then, Tomás? The same thing you did before?"

Tomás said nothing. He could not say anything. Marco came closer and said, with small words: "I know what happened in the large place. I know what you did. And I want this: you tell it to all the people here yourself. You yourself say it. If you do not say it, I say it. You do not have much time." Tomás went toward his place, moving like a dead person who moves.

After this, Tomás could not be well. He thought one thing, and thought it all the time: This person knows. How can he know? Only two people knew what happened — me and… No. It cannot be. The dead do not come. The dead do not speak. The dead do not know. But he also thought another thing, and this thing was like something alive inside him: Did anyone see him die? No one. No one saw him die.

Rosa could not stay inside her place. She thought: Perhaps now I can see him. Perhaps now he tells me more. She went to the place where Marco was. He saw her coming, and for a moment — a very small moment — his body moved like the body of Daniel moved when he saw Rosa, a long time before. She saw this. She saw it and felt something very large inside.

She said to him: "When you saw me just now, something happened inside you. I saw it. Daniel used to move like that when he saw me. Who are you? Say it. If you are Daniel, say it to me. Not to the people — to me." Marco said nothing for a long time. Then he said: "If I say: I am Daniel, what happens? You want your Daniel to live. But the Daniel you want is dead, Rosa. He died when he left this place. The person who is here now knows things that that Daniel did not know. Saw things that that Daniel did not see. A person can die and live, and not be the same person."

Rosa said: "I do not want large words. I want one small word: yes or no." And Marco looked at her — looked at her as Daniel used to look at her, a long time before — and said: "I want this: all the people know the truth about Tomás. After that, you are going to know the truth about me. The two truths are parts of one thing."

Marco said no more, and Rosa went toward her place. The night was in everything. And inside her, all the time, the words she could not not think moved: People say that Daniel is dead. But no one saw him die. No one.
