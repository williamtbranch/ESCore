## Character & Style Bible (visual — keep identical in every illustration prompt)

> This block is the source of truth for the prompt generator. Copy it verbatim
> into the header of every episode's prompt file.

- **Rosa** — 22, a blend of Saoirse Ronan and Lily Collins: fair freckled skin,
  long auburn hair, large green eyes; faded blue gingham dress with a cream
  apron and worn leather boots.
- **Marco / Daniel** — 25, a blend of a young Oscar Isaac and Diego Luna: olive
  skin, dark wavy hair, a short dark beard, intense brown eyes; a dark
  traveler's coat over a collarless linen shirt. Marco is the living image of
  the dead Daniel; in any reflection of the past, Daniel is ~20, clean-shaven,
  in a plain white shirt.
- **Inés** — 72, a blend of Anjelica Huston and Rita Moreno: deeply lined olive
  face, silver hair pulled back, dark knowing eyes; a long charcoal-grey dress
  and a black fringed shawl.
- **Tomás** — 32, a blend of Casey Affleck and a young Adam Driver: gaunt pale
  face, dark hair greying at the temples, hollow restless eyes; a rumpled brown
  work jacket over an open-collared shirt.
- **Setting** — a small remote village of rough stone-and-timber houses with
  terracotta roofs and dusty lanes, ringed by dry golden hills; warm earthy
  palette, early-1900s rural feel.
- **Style** — classic oil painting in the style of Art Nouveau.

## Premise

A man who is the living image of the dead Daniel arrives in a remote village
and calls himself Marco. Daniel's death was only ever words: Tomás announced
it years ago, claiming to have heard it far away. Nobody saw the body. Nobody
saw him die. The series' real subject is **belief** — a death that exists only
because words made it exist, and what happens when the village must stop
believing.

## The Three Secrets (spend slowly — one per stretch of episodes)

| # | Secret | Holder | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **What Tomás did** in the lugar grande the night Daniel "died" | Tomás (Marco knows; Inés saw "everything") | OPEN — confession begun and cut at end of Ep 2; full recounting banked for Ep 3+ |
| 2 | **Why Inés stayed silent** for years | Inés | SPENT in Ep 2 — she stayed silent to protect Rosa, not Tomás; loving complicity, now in collision with Rosa's wish to know |
| 3 | **Who Marco is / how Daniel survived** (if he did) | Marco | OPEN — heavily implied, never confirmed; Marco has promised Rosa the truth after Tomás's truth is public ("las dos verdades son partes de una cosa") |

**Rule:** never resolve two secrets in the same episode. The series engine dies
when the last secret closes — the finale spends Secret 3.

## Character Arcs

### Rosa — from questioner to agent
- Ep 1: asks for truth, escalates rhetorically, holds her card (*ahora no*).
- Ep 2: **spends her secret** — confronts Tomás with what Inés revealed, acting
  before Marco's timetable and breaking his control of events.
- Arc direction: each episode must give her a decision with cost, not just a
  stronger question. End-state: she forces the final truth on her own terms,
  and must decide whether the man in front of her is *her* Daniel at all —
  Marco's own warning (*el Daniel que tú quieres está muerto*) is the trap her
  arc walks into.

### Marco / Daniel — from oracle to man
- Ep 1: omniscient, static, visited by everyone; one crack (his body moves like
  Daniel's when he sees Rosa).
- Ep 2: **loses control of his plan** when Rosa acts independently; one moment
  of visible uncertainty. His want sharpens: public truth about Tomás first,
  himself second.
- Arc direction: progressively de-mystify. Every episode must cost him
  something or surprise him once. His vengeance frame should crack against
  Rosa: revenge wants the past public, Rosa wants the past *over*.
- Open question to answer late: did he come back for justice, for Rosa, or
  because a "dead" man has nowhere else to be?

### Tomás — the guilty man under a clock
- Ep 1: dread on sight of Marco; lie restated under Rosa's questioning; given
  an ultimatum at night.
- Ep 2: the ultimatum becomes public with a **two-day deadline**; he tries to
  escape through Inés, is refused, is confronted by Rosa, and finally stands
  before the village — confession cut mid-sentence.
- Arc direction: his guilt is shown somatically (the register's best tool).
  Whatever he did must turn out *smaller and more human* than the village
  imagines — cowardice, jealousy of Daniel's leaving, an accident, an
  abandonment — not a cartoon murder. The horror is that words did the killing.

### Inés — the keeper whose love was complicity
- Ep 1: warnings, refusals, the night visit to Marco (he once promised her
  never to return).
- Ep 2: **motive revealed** — her silence was protection of Rosa, not of Tomás.
  This puts her in direct moral collision with Rosa, who declared she would
  rather be destroyed than ignorant.
- Arc direction: she has seen "everything" (Marco's claim, Ep 1) — she is the
  only living witness besides the two who left. Her final function: arbiter of
  the last truth, and the series' moral voice on whether some words should
  ever be said.

## Established Continuity Facts (do not contradict)

1. Daniel and Rosa shared secret words she never repeated to anyone (Ep 1).
2. Daniel left the village wanting "big places, big things."
3. **Daniel and Tomás left together, the same night; Tomás returned alone**
   (Inés's disclosure, Ep 1).
4. Tomás announced the death claiming he was far away and was told of it.
5. Nobody in the village saw the body; nobody saw him die.
6. Marco once told Inés he would *never* come to this place (Ep 1) — so Inés
   has met him before, far from the village, and knew him well enough for
   promises.
7. Marco knows what Tomás did and says Inés "saw it all and said nothing."
8. Marco's ultimatum (Ep 1, private): tell the village or I will. Made public
   with a two-day deadline in Ep 2.
9. Marco neither confirms nor denies being Daniel; he argues identity is not
   binary (*una persona puede morir y vivir, y no ser la misma persona*).

## Refrains (series-level, reuse intentionally)

- *Nadie lo vio morir.* — the title line; close episodes near it when possible.
- *Nadie lo vio, pero todos lo creyeron.* — the belief refrain (from Ep 2 on,
  built on the unlocked word `creer`).
- *Palabras pequeñas.* — intimacy and secrets.
- Somatic truth: bodies say what mouths will not (*su cuerpo decía otra cosa*).
